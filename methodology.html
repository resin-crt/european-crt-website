Typology Methodology.

A risk based approach

The Climate Risk Typology is formed around the risk framework applied within the latest report released by the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), the 5th Assessment Report. Here, risk is a function of climate hazards, and exposure and vulnerability (which encompasses sensitivity and adaptive capacity) to these hazards. The Typology is based around a set of indicators that reflect these risk elements.

Typology spatial scale

The Climate Risk Typology is formed at the scale of European NUTS3 regions, which are defined as ‘small regions’. NUTS3 regions are part of a system that subdivides the economic territory of Europe to support statistical data gathering, socio-economic analysis and the framing of European policies. There are 1342 NUTS3 regions in Europe. NUTS3 regions are a population-based classification system, and contain between 150,000 – 800,000 people. As a result, the density of NUTS3 regions across Europe varies. For example, there are 402 in Germany, 21 in Sweden and 5 in the city-region of Greater Manchester (located in North West England).
