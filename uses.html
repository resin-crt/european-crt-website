Uses for the typology.

Principal uses of the Typology

  • Visualisation, description and communication of climate risk: The typology can be used to communicate and raise awareness of climate risk, and the different factors that drive climate risk, amongst different stakeholder groups. This can help to generate commitment for adaptation and resilience action.
  • Climate change adaptation strategy development and planning: A key benefit of the typology, and the indicator data underpinning the typology, is in supporting climate change adaptation strategy and planning. For example, the indicator data, which covers hazard, exposure and vulnerability themes, can be accessed and used to inform risk assessment exercises. Also, the typology can inform strategic planning and highlight climate risk issues of particular relevance to specific European cities and regions.
  • Developing networks of European cities and regions: The typology presents the opportunity to identify European cities and regions with similar climate risk characteristics. This can aid the development of networks between areas that face common challenges in order to encourage the sharing of experience and good practice.
  • Climate risk ‘baselining’: The typology provides a ‘snapshot’ of climate risk in European NUTS3 regions based on the climate and socio-economic indicator data available at the time of its development. As climate projections data is updated, or urban socio-economic conditions change, the indicator data underpinning the typology will change. Further typology analysis could be undertaken to determine the effect of these changes on climate risk. The risk ‘snapshot’ (or baseline) produced within the RESIN project therefore provides the opportunity to assess changes in climate risk across the European landscape over time.

Potential Typology Users

The Climate Risk Typology will be most useful for three core user groups:
Regional, national and European planners and decision-makers. Potential applications of the typology at these scales include:

  • The typology enables users to better understand climate risk and the factors underlying climate risk. It also supports the communication of these issues visually, and the development of a strategic perspective on spatial patterns of climate risk.
  • The typology can assist the development of regional, national and European scale adaptation and resilience strategies and plans. The typology output and the climate risk indicator data represent a valuable data resource that can inform these tasks.
  • Where capacity and resources to support urban adaptation and resilience activity are limited, the typology outputs can be used help to target resources to particular areas of need or opportunity.
  • The typology output can aid the creation of peer-to-peer learning networks between European cities and regions, with the goal of sharing and strengthening good practice.

Urban planners and decision makers engaged in developing climate change adaptation and resilience strategies.
  • The typology can be used by urban planners and decision makers to help make the case for, and develop commitment to creating and implementing, adaptation and resilience strategies and actions.
  • The typology can inform the development of strategies and plans to reduce climate risks. By helping to identify the climate risk characteristics of European cities and urban areas, appropriate strategies and actions can be developed in response. Here, the typology can help to prioritise locally relevant climate change adaptation objectives to incorporate within strategies and plans.
  • The typology presents urban decision makers with the opportunity to develop contacts and networks amongst cities with similar climate risk profiles to share and gain knowledge and experience.

Researchers working on climate change adaptation and resilience and related topics:
  • The typology provides a new way of visualising and analysing climate risk in Europe. It also demonstrates and describes methodological developments related to the creation and use of climate risk indicators at the European scale.

Typology use cases

The typology portal will contain several use cases to guide the application of the typology in practice. The focus of the use cases is yet to be defined. Options for use cases include:

  • Using the typology at different spatial scales.
  • Using the typology to support specific elements of the adaptation planning process.
  • Use cases related to specific applications will be considered, for example to support meeting the adaptation requirements of the Covenant of Majors.
